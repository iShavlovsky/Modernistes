The Collection

Our collection focuses on the most important French postwar designers that shaped the modernist movement globally. These include Charlotte Perriand, Jean Prouvé and Le Corbusier, with an emphasis on rare items found in reputable auctions from established collections.

Jean Prouvé

Determined to be a man of his time, Prouvé explored new technological advances to produce new solutions for design, fabrication. He contributed to the reconstruction and urbanization of France after the war. His fascination with innovation allowed him to break away from traditional means of construction while giving priority to experience over profit. The Prouvé blend of avant-garde spirit and humanist concerns has lost none of its relevance.

Pierre Jeanneret

The Swiss-born architect and furniture designer worked for most of his life alongside his cousin Le Corbusier – perhaps most notably on a massive urban planning project in Chandigarh, India, where Jeanneret designed and produced furniture to furnish a state capital.

Charlotte Perriand

Perriand was one of the most iconic designers of the modernist era, working with both the Le Corbusier studio (alongside Pierre Jeanneret) and later on, Jean Prouve. Her work aimed to create functional living spaces in the belief that better design helps in creating a better society.

Serge Mouille

Trained as a goldsmith, Mouille was part of the avant-garde of French design. Wishing to produce goldsmith's work for the table, he turned to lighting in 1952 thanks to a commission from the Compagnie des Arts Français. In spite of an always more important demand, he will always keep the will to preserve a craft production. His luminaries are today recognizable among all.
